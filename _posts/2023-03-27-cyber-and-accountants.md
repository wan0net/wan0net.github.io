#Professional Accreditations
The concept of professional accreditations has been around on an individual basis for many many years. State and Federal Governments around the world enshrine the requirements for professional standards within legislation, ensuring in fields with paricular risk situations, that workers are required to be accredited by some form of professional standards body. Lawyers are required to "submit to the bar" in order to be a practicing lawyer and support you in court. 

This is even stretches to organisations - for example organisations in Australian States that offer gambling products - lotteries, pokies, etc... - must undergo registration and stringent checks in order to ensure that everything is above board. The same applies to charities - in order to be a registered charity in Australia there are certain requirements you must meet in terms of governance, financial responsibilities, and reporting.
