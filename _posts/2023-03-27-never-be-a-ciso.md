I never want to be a CISO.

now let’s ignore for a second that not all CISOs are created equally - the roles, responsibility and maturity for a CISO for a two person company is not the same as a 1000+ or even 100,000+ company by any stretch of the imagination, and you cannot just transplant someone from one to the other and expect the same results.

But being a CISO is just a lot of heartache. And I get some people want that prestige, and responsibility. But it’s not the be all and end all.

You need to work out what’s best for you from what I’ve been describing as three legs of a stool. Work, health, relationships. Succeeding in one, can cause the stool to become overbalanced. Having issues in another can lead to the same effect.

You should always consider what you want based on your circumstances. And that won’t be the same as everyone else. I guarantee that 50% at least of those people who say they want to be a CISO don’t know what that means, cannot articulate what scale, roles or responsibilities. And you need to understand that detail because it affects the other stool legs
